# AWIG OS

**An operating system that follows a thousand-year-old way of governing.**

AWIG OS is a free, open-source project — no company, no product, no trademark, no money. It exists because powerful AI is being deployed faster than it is being governed, and because the answer to that problem was not invented in a lab. It was practiced, for about a thousand years, in the rice terraces of Bali.

---

## 1. The water and the rulebook

On the volcanic slopes of Bali, water is life and water is shared. For roughly a thousand years, farming communities called **subak** have governed that shared water together — not through a king's decree, not through a corporation's terms of service, but through their own institutions: water temples as meeting points, deliberation as method, ritual as protocol. In 2012, UNESCO inscribed the subak landscape as a World Heritage of humanity.

Each subak, and each Balinese village, writes and keeps its own rulebook: the ***awig-awig***.

The awig-awig is a constitution written **by the community it governs**. It is:

- **Self-written** — the people who live under the rules are the people who make them;
- **Legible** — written down, readable by every member, not hidden in anyone's head or vault;
- **Amended in the open** — changed by known process, in front of everyone;
- **Binding because it is shared** — its force comes from common acceptance, not from imposition.

Water flows through everyone's fields. Rules are written by everyone's hands. Decisions are made where everyone can see. That is the philosophy. It kept a complex, life-critical, shared system running for a millennium — through empires, colonizers, and modernity — without ever being owned by anyone.

This project holds that the same philosophy is the right way — perhaps the only honest way — to govern the most powerful shared flow of our own time: **computation and AI**.

## 2. How this project follows that philosophy

AWIG OS is the attempt to build that philosophy into the foundation of a computer system. Not as a feature. As the ground.

**Rules are self-written.** Every deployment of AWIG OS writes and keeps its own machine-readable rulebook — its own awig-awig. The system does not tell you what your rules should be. It guarantees that your rules are yours, written, and real.

**Rules are legible.** All rules in the system share one common, readable, executable format. There is no privileged secret syntax for privileged actors. What governs you can be read by you.

**Power flows in the open.** Like water through the terraces, every exercise of permission in the system leaves a visible trace. Authority that leaves no trace does not run. The powerful are not asked to be good; they are structured to be seen.

**Intelligence is governed, not worshipped.** AI inside AWIG OS lives as a governed community, not an unaccountable oracle — bounded, inspectable, constitutional, with the human owner above the whole, the way the subak's members stand above their own institutions.

**The commons stays common.** This project takes no trademark, sells nothing, and is licensed so that no one — however large — can take it, close it, and sell it back to the world with the accountability stripped out. The license is this project's own first rule: written down, binding on everyone, heaviest on the strongest. (See [`LICENSING.md`](./LICENSING.md).)

## 3. Acknowledgment

This project is named in homage to the ***awig-awig*** tradition of Bali and Lombok, Indonesia, and to the **subak** communities who have practiced governance-as-a-commons for a thousand years. It also honors Indonesia's broader heritage of deliberative, communal decision-making — *musyawarah untuk mufakat*, deliberation toward consensus.

These traditions are not decoration on this project. They are its argument: that legible, self-written, openly-practiced governance of a shared vital flow is not utopian — it is one of the most proven institutional designs in human history. The world should know where it comes from.

Contributors from Indonesia — and corrections to how this heritage is described — are especially and permanently welcome. Documentation in Bahasa Indonesia is a stated goal of this project.

## 5. How this project governs itself

A project about governance must keep its own awig-awig, visibly:

- [`GOVERNANCE.md`](./GOVERNANCE.md) — how decisions about this project are proposed, discussed, and made; amendable only by the process it describes.
- All decisions of consequence are recorded in the open — including rejections. A community that keeps its refusals honest keeps its acceptances meaningful.
- Until a contributor community forms, the project runs as a single maintainer with a full written trace, transitioning to community governance as participation grows — the way a rulebook grows with its village.

## 6. The machine

The technical design — what AWIG OS actually is as software, how it is being rebuilt from a Linux reference model, its rule engine, its AI organisation — lives in [`ARCHITECTURE.md`](./ARCHITECTURE.md).

The theory this system implements has its own home: the [**Open Governance Framework**](https://github.com/kfkchau/open-governance-framework) — the definitive governance theory infrastructure, under which the full body of work sits (concepts and ontology, the rule lifecycle, the [Open Meta-Governance Standard (OMGS)](https://github.com/kfkchau/Open-Meta-Governance-Standard/), and the standards that follow). AWIG OS is that theory, running.

The philosophy comes first in this README because it comes first in the system.

**Status:** pre-alpha, design and reference-model stage. The first milestone is deliberately small: one rule executes in the common format, one permission runs and leaves a verifiable trace, and a stranger can independently verify both. Everything grows from that seed.
